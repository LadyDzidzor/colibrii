10 Tasks you should be outsourcing regularly

April 19

For a large corporation, a small business or a solopreneur, the demands of managing the day-to-day running of your business can seem overwhelming. You might be hard-pressed with time, money, and human resource. You might also find yourself being more interested in the creative side of your business. This should not stop your business from reaching its visions or smashing daily, weekly or monthly goals. Outsource your business tasks to hit those goals faster because you cannot do it all by yourself. Here is a list of 10 tasks you should be outsourcing regularly:


1. Bookeeping


Except your company is a bookkeeping company or you are a financial guru, you might not be interested in the financial records that keep a business running. You can do the bookkeeping yourself but it will take your time – time you could have invested in other aspects of your business. There are virtual assistants with financial skill sets. You can contract this to them and they will take care of all your messy financial work.


2. Email Management


Email management Everyone hates the boring monotonous routine of “best regards” and “find attached”, and the frustrating ordeal of scanning through thousands of emails and prioritizing which to reply to and how to reply to each of them. Why do you keep on doing this when it is not saving you any time or money? According to a study by Adobe, on average, you can spend six (6) hours a day doing this. Outsourcing the task of email management would help you regain the time you lose each day.


3. Customer Feedback


The customer is always right. Therefore, it is important to factor customer feedback into your business model. However, you don’t have to do it yourself especially if you have a large customer base. It would leave you no room to act on that feedback. You can outsource this task to a virtual assistant who can respond and forward the concerns of your customers. Your business will thank you for this when you outsource it.


4. Travel Management


Every now and then, you might need to travel for both business and personal purposes. The choice of a means of travel and other decisions like accommodation, food, guide, etc. can be overwhelming and can take a significant part of your time. Let a virtual assistant handle it for you.


5. Data Entry


If you run a project and need to store a large amount of data, it would be very useful to let someone else enter that data on your behalf. Virtual assistants can help you do that conveniently, helping you speed up the completion of your projects.


6. Schedule and Calendar management


Someone has to book appointments and cancel them. If your business model relies on face-to-face or virtual meetings, you would easily lose yourself trying to catch up with the number, date and time of appointments. You can outsource this to a virtual assistant and free up your mind for what to say at your meetings!


7. Database management


Except you are a database manager, you might be better off managing your time by allowing a virtual assistant manage your database while freeing up your time for more productive work. Even if you were a database manager, you would still need some help in managing several databases. A virtual assistant can help you do this effectively.


8. Digital marketing


Digital content is what drives businesses online. Digital marketing is broad and can range from Search Engine Optimization (SEO), Search Engine Marketing (SEM), paid social media advertising, influencer marketing, content marketing and a wide range of others. Each of these would need an expert and you cannot do all by yourself. Outsource them.


9. Managing websites


There is so much involved when it comes to managing your business website or your personal website. From building the website to managing the content on it and renewing domain and hosting plans, you can get frustrated or overwhelmed. This is the reason you should outsource this.


10. Content formatting and editing


Business managers and solopreneurs are mostly perfectionists and like to manage their work by themselves but having a fresh eye go over your written work to provide proper edits is always better. By outsourcing this task, you would be able to catch errors that your eyes must have skipped when you have a different person helping you with content editing and formatting.


Conclusion


Knowing when to seek help and when to let go is a skill you can cultivate. Outsourcing your tasks is one of the best ways to seek help and let go of the tasks you should be outsourcing regularly.
